Detailed Description

Cell mechanics properties definition for cancer simulation.

Author
Luciana Melina Luque

This file defines the Mecanica (Mechanics) class, which encapsulates the mechanical properties of cells that govern physical interactions with other cells, the extracellular matrix, and boundary surfaces. These properties determine how cells adhere to and repel each other, forming the basis for tissue architecture.

In cancer research, mechanical properties are critically important as they influence:

  • Tumor compactness and organization
  • Invasive capacity and metastatic potential
  • Response to mechanical stresses in the tumor microenvironment
  • Physical barriers to drug delivery
  • Mechanical cues that can trigger phenotypic changes
